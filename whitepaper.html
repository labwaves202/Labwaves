Labwaves — Whitepaper

Labwaves is an experimental media format at the intersection of music, research, and space. It explores how scientific and cultural contexts can be connected through sound, conversation, and atmosphere.

What is Labwaves?

Labwaves is a hybrid audio-visual format that combines short research-oriented conversations with musical performance. Instead of separating academic content from cultural expression, the project brings both into a shared setting.

Why this format?

Research is often communicated in highly formal ways. Labwaves experiments with a different approach: one that is accessible, atmospheric, and emotionally resonant. Music does not serve as decoration, but as a medium that opens attention, curiosity, and new forms of connection.

How it works

A typical Labwaves session combines a short interview, a specific spatial context, and a live or recorded music performance. The aim is to create a compact format that makes ideas, environments, and practices perceptible in a different way.

What it connects

Labwaves connects artistic practice, research culture, technical environments, and public communication. It is interested in transitions between disciplines, institutions, and audiences rather than in fixed categories.

Future development

The format is intentionally open and can evolve into live sessions, recorded episodes, collaborations with researchers and artists, or site-specific experiments in different environments. Labwaves is conceived as an adaptable platform rather than a fixed template.

Open invitation

Labwaves welcomes collaboration with people interested in sound, research, experimentation, and new forms of public engagement.
